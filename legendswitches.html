Back Web Design • Motion Graphics

Making premium electrical hardware feel as considered as the spaces it lives in.

Legend Switch makes high-quality, stylish electrical switches and sockets for homes and offices. The product is premium, durable, and design-forward — but the digital presence wasn’t keeping pace. I was brought in to build a landing page and motion graphics system that could finally show the product the way it deserved to be shown.

The Intro

Legend Switch sits in an interesting product category. Electrical switches are everywhere — every room, every wall, every building — but they're almost never considered as design objects. They're afterthoughts. Legend Switch is trying to change that, making hardware that people actually notice and choose deliberately, the way they choose a light fixture or a door handle.

The brief was to build a digital presence that reflected that positioning. A landing page and motion graphics system that could communicate quality, elegance, and durability in a format that works on screens — for a product that ultimately lives on walls.

The Challenge

The electrical hardware category has a presentation problem. Most players in the space — even premium ones — rely on catalogue-style product photography and specification tables. The result is a category that feels functional but never desirable. Legend Switch's products are genuinely well-designed, and the digital presence needed to communicate that without borrowing the aesthetic language of brands they weren't trying to be.

The specific challenges on the table:

  • Translate a physical, tactile product into a compelling digital experience.
  • Build a landing page that communicates premium quality without tipping into cold or corporate.
  • Create motion graphics that make a switch — a deliberately understated object — feel worth paying attention to.
  • Establish a visual language that could scale into a broader content and marketing system over time.
  • Make the website feel like a destination, not a brochure.

Kick Off

The brief centred on one core insight: people who care about interior design care about every detail. The switch on the wall isn't background noise — it's a finishing touch, and the right one matters. Legend Switch's customers are the people who notice that kind of thing. The digital presence had to speak directly to that sensibility.

We established the creative direction early. Premium, warm, and tactile — a visual language that referenced high-end interiors and considered design without losing the accessibility that makes Legend Switch relevant across different home and office contexts. The motion work would follow the same principle: elegant, unhurried, product-forward.

Key decisions made at kick-off:

  • Warm, neutral palette — tones that reference interior design moodboards rather than electronics catalogues.
  • Typography anchored in clean, contemporary display type with enough personality to carry the brand voice.
  • Product photography as the hero — every layout decision in service of making the hardware look as good as it is.
  • Motion system built for social distribution: short, loopable, designed to perform in feed formats.

The Process

The work ran across two parallel tracks: the landing page and the motion graphics.

The landing page was structured around a simple editorial logic: introduce the brand, show the product, communicate the quality, give people a reason to stay. The hero section leads with the product in context — not isolated on a white background, but living in the kind of space it was designed for. Subsequent sections move through the product range, the brand values, and the craftsmanship story, all structured to build the case without ever feeling like a hard sell.

Layout decisions were made with the product's natural scale in mind. Switches are small objects — they need room to breathe on screen. Generous spacing, large imagery, and a restrained colour palette all work together to let the hardware command attention without the page working against it.

The motion graphics track was built to complement the landing page and extend the brand's presence into social content. Each piece was designed around a product moment: the satisfying snap of a switch, the subtle texture of a socket face, the clean geometry of a well-made fitting in a well-designed room. Short, loopable, and built to make a small object feel significant.

Landing Page

The Legend Switch landing page is built to feel like a premium interior brand, not an electrical supplier. Every section earns its place by moving the visitor closer to understanding why these switches are worth choosing deliberately.

Motion Graphics

A motion piece built to show Legend Switch's product range in its best light — designed for social distribution and built to make hardware feel like a design object worth wanting.

Closer

Legend Switch is the kind of project that tests your ability to find the extraordinary in the ordinary. A switch is a small thing. The work of making it feel significant — on a screen, in motion, across a landing page — requires genuine creative conviction.

The outcome is a digital presence that finally matches the quality of the product. A landing page that feels considered, and a motion system that makes hardware worth watching. That's the job, and I'm proud of how it came together.
