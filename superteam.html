Superteam Bounties: Designing for Solana Ecosystem Products.

Over the past year, I participated in multiple bounty challenges across the Solana ecosystem through the Superteam Nigeria and Superteam Ukraine communities. These projects challenged me to communicate complex crypto products through motion design, product storytelling, and web experiences that felt approachable to everyday users. Out of my submitted bounty projects, two were selected as winning submissions, validating both the creative direction and strategic thinking behind the work.

The Challenge

One recurring problem across Web3 products is communication. Many products solve real problems but struggle to explain their value in a way that feels clear, trustworthy, and memorable. The goal across each bounty was simple: Turn technical products into stories people could immediately understand.

This required balancing:

  • Product education
  • Motion design
  • User attention
  • Brand personality
  • Conversion-focused storytelling

Project 01: Rhiva

Making Liquidity Provision Feel Understandable: Rhiva is a liquidity provision platform designed to help users participate in decentralized financial markets more efficiently. The challenge wasn't the technology. The challenge was explaining liquidity provision without overwhelming viewers with DeFi terminology.

Approach: Instead of focusing on protocols and mechanics, I focused on outcomes. The motion design centered around capital flowing through ecosystems, liquidity movement, network effects, and growth through participation. Complex financial concepts were translated into visual systems that felt intuitive and easy to follow.

Result: The final animation transformed a highly technical service into a clear narrative that users could understand within seconds. The project demonstrated how motion can simplify finance without sacrificing accuracy. The submission was selected among standout entries within the bounty program.

Project 02: Xpend

Showing the Fastest Route from Crypto to Naira: Xpend helps users convert cryptocurrency into Naira quickly and efficiently. The challenge was familiar to many Nigerian crypto users: People understand crypto. They often don't understand the safest and fastest way to spend it in everyday life.

Approach: The motion narrative focused on a single journey: Crypto -> Conversion -> Spending Power. Rather than showcasing features first, the animation followed the user's perspective. Key areas highlighted:

  • Speed
  • Simplicity
  • Accessibility
  • Real-world utility

The visual language emphasized movement, flow, and instant value delivery.

Result: The final piece communicated Xpend's value proposition in a format that was engaging, educational, and conversion-focused.

Project 03: Ranktasy

Turning Competition Into Entertainment: Ranktasy sits at the intersection of gaming, predictions, and community engagement. The challenge was capturing excitement before users even interacted with the platform.

Approach: The motion system focused on rankings, leaderboards, progression, competition, and rewards. Every sequence was designed to create anticipation and reinforce the feeling of climbing toward the top. The visual direction balanced playfulness with product credibility.

Result: The final animation helped communicate the platform's energy and competitive nature while remaining aligned with the broader Solana ecosystem.

Project 04: Superteam Ukraine

Designing for a Global Builder Community: For Superteam Ukraine, I submitted a website design concept focused on community participation and ecosystem growth. The challenge was designing a platform that could attract Builders, Designers, Developers, Founders, and Contributors while maintaining a strong sense of community identity.

Approach: The website focused on clear information hierarchy, community-first storytelling, ecosystem visibility, contributor onboarding, and opportunity discovery. The design aimed to make participation feel accessible while showcasing the impact of community-led innovation.

Result: The concept demonstrated how thoughtful web design can strengthen engagement and improve discovery across ecosystem initiatives.

"It's about making complex products feel human."
— Working across multiple bounty programs

Closer

Working across multiple bounty programs taught me that successful Web3 design is rarely about making things look futuristic. It's about making complex products feel human. Across motion design and web experiences, the focus remained consistent:

  • Simplify complexity.
  • Build trust quickly.
  • Educate without overwhelming.
  • Turn product features into memorable stories.

Outcomes: Multiple bounty submissions completed across the Solana ecosystem. Winning submissions in 2 bounty challenges. Motion graphics, product storytelling, and web design experience across finance, payments, gaming, and community platforms. Collaboration within global Solana communities through Superteam Nigeria and Superteam Ukraine.
