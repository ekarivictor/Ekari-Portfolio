Project 05: Casino Von

Bringing a casino to life before it ever opened its doors. Casino Von is a mid-range casino experience located in Enugu, Nigeria. Before the first chip was placed or the first drink was poured, they needed to look the part. I was brought in to build the pre-launch visual identity — a collection of branded graphics, room artwork, and print collateral that would set the tone for what Casino Von was going to feel like the moment guests walked in.

THE INTRO: Pre-launch creative work carries a specific kind of pressure. There is no product to reference, no customer feedback to draw from, no established atmosphere to match. There is only the vision — and the job of translating it into something people can see, feel, and get excited about before the doors open.

For Casino Von, that meant building a visual world from the ground up. Graphics for the launch campaign, artwork for the physical rooms, and brand flyers that could travel ahead of the space and do the work of making people curious. Everything built to signal one thing clearly: something worth showing up for is coming.

The Challenge

Enugu's entertainment scene is competitive on the ground but visually underserved at the top. Most venues in the space lean on generic international casino aesthetics — gold, red, playing card motifs, the usual shorthand. Casino Von needed to stand apart without alienating a mid-range audience that still wanted to feel like they were stepping into something elevated.

The challenge was calibration. Too raw and the brand wouldn't communicate the experience. Too polished and it would feel disconnected from the Enugu audience it was actually trying to reach. The creative direction had to find the exact register between aspirational and accessible — a casino that felt exciting and worth dressing up for, without feeling like it was designed for someone else.

Specific problems on the table:

  • Build a visual identity strong enough to anchor all pre-launch materials without a fully formalised brand system behind it.
  • Design room artwork that felt considered and venue-appropriate, not decorative filler.
  • Create launch graphics and flyers that could move on social media and in print simultaneously.
  • Establish a visual tone that would survive the transition from pre-launch hype to the permanent in-venue atmosphere.

Kick Off

The brief centred on one deliverable above all others: make Casino Von look like a place people would want to go before they had any reason to believe it was real. Pre-launch marketing lives or dies on visual credibility. The creative direction had to carry enough weight to do that job.

We aligned on a dark, rich aesthetic — deep tones, deliberate contrast, typography that felt both premium and grounded. The visual language would borrow from the atmosphere of well-executed hospitality spaces: moody, inviting, and specific enough to feel like it belonged to Casino Von and nowhere else.

Key decisions made at kick-off:

  • Dark-first palette anchored in deep, atmospheric tones with high-contrast accent moments.
  • Typography chosen for legibility across both digital formats and large-format print.
  • Artwork direction built around the physical rooms — pieces designed to inhabit space, not just decorate it.
  • Flyer and graphic system flexible enough to serve announcements, event promotions, and general brand awareness from a single visual framework.

The Process & Work

The work split across three tracks running in parallel: room artwork, brand graphics, and print collateral.

Room Artwork: A curated collection of pieces designed for Casino Von's physical interiors. Dark, atmospheric, and built for the specific proportions and lighting conditions of a casino environment. The collection functions as a whole — individual pieces that read individually but contribute to a unified room-level atmosphere. Not art for art's sake, but art doing the work of making a room feel intentional.

Brand Graphics: Pre-launch social and digital assets built to communicate Casino Von's identity before the venue was open. Campaign graphics, announcement visuals, and branded content designed to travel on social media while maintaining print-level quality. The system was built for longevity — flexible enough to serve an ongoing content calendar, consistent enough to build recognition fast.

Closer

Casino Von is a pre-launch project with a post-launch future. The work built here — the room pieces, the graphics, the flyers — was always meant to be a foundation, not a ceiling. A starting point for a visual identity that would grow as the venue grew, accumulate meaning as the space accumulated memories, and eventually feel like it had always belonged there.

That's the best outcome for this kind of work. Not just something that looked good on launch day, but something that holds up as the place becomes real and the brand becomes lived in. I'm proud of what we put together for Casino Von, and I'm watching to see what it becomes.
